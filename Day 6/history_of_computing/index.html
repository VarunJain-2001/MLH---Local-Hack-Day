  • The Difference Engine No.2 Desinged by Charles Babbage

    The Difference Engine was designed by Charles Babbage in the 1820's and completely built by the London science museum in 1991. It was designed to solve equations and print them out on a mathematical table, it was one of two types of 'engine' Babbage designed. The difference engine & analytical engine.

  • Tabulating Machine

    The Tabulating Machine was created by Herman Hollerith in 1888 to be used to process the data in the 1890 U.S. Census. The punched card also used in the tabulating machine that was also invented by Hollerith, the tabulating machine read and summarised the information the punched card, later models were used for accounting and inventory.

  • The Turing Welchman Bombe

    The Turing Welchman Bombe machine first started code breaking in 1940. The machine was designed to break the german enigma code, used by the Nazi german military to communicate to it's various branches of military without the allies being able to understand what was being said. By the end of the war just under 1800 people were involved in deploying 211 bombe machines.

  • The World Wide Web

    The World Wide Web was developed at CERN, initially to meet the demand for information sharing universities and scientific institutions. Then Tim Berners-Lee proposed the idea of the world wide web which he described as, "a hypertext project" called "WorldWideWeb" in which a "web" of "hypertext documents" could be viewed by “browsers”". Then in 1990 Berners-Lee started his first web server and web browser at CERN. The first website is still live and can be visited by anyone click here to go to that website.

  • The USB Flash Drive

    The USB flash drive was created in 1999 it was built on flash memory technology hence the name "flash" drive. Flash uses MOSFET transistors as memory cells, a time went on the read and write speeds were improved along with the storage space they were capable of. The "USB" part of the name refers the plug that goes into a device, that has also been improved over time going to 2.0 to 3.0 etc. Now the latest iteration of that is USB C which now looks like is slowly becoming the standard for flash drives and general connection to devices.

  • Bitcoin (Cryptocurrency)

    Bitcoin is considered to be the first cryptocurrency released in 2009. Bitcoin's underlying technology is called blockchain, blockchain is a shared network that "facilitates the process of recording transactions and tracking assests in a business network". According to IBM's definition. The same people that made bitcoin are the same people that created blockchain as that is the tech that essentially bitcoin is "run on". Bitcoin is a 'decentralised' digital currency users can anonymously transfer bitcoin without and third party inteference like banks or currency, however even though this is the case blockchain is still very secure as there is no one place where data is stored, making it hard for hackers to pinpoint a place to hack (for example).
